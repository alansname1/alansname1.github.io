What Is Considered a Good Typing Speed?

A good typing speed depends on your goals, experience level, and the type of work you do. For many people, typing around 40 words per minute is considered average and practical for everyday computer use. Higher typing speeds may help with office work, remote jobs, school assignments, customer service, writing, and data entry tasks.

A strong typing score is not only about speed. Accuracy also matters. Typing quickly with many mistakes can slow down your real productivity because corrections take extra time.

Simple benchmark: around 40 WPM is often considered average, 50–60 WPM is generally considered good, and 70+ WPM is strong when accuracy is also high.

Why Typing Speed Matters

Typing speed can affect how quickly you complete tasks, respond to messages, write documents, fill out forms, and handle computer-based work. Faster and more accurate typing can save time and make work feel smoother and less frustrating.

Typing skill is useful for students, office workers, customer service representatives, remote workers, writers, administrative assistants, and anyone who spends time using a computer.

What Typing Speed Is Good for Jobs?

Many office and administrative jobs may expect typing around 40 to 60 WPM. Data entry, transcription, customer support, and typing-heavy remote work may prefer even higher speed with strong accuracy.

General Office Work

40–50 WPM is often useful for emails, forms, reports, and everyday tasks.

Customer Service

50–60 WPM can help with chat support, notes, and customer communication.

Data Entry

50–70 WPM may be useful for entering records and forms accurately.

Remote Work

Typing confidence helps with messaging, documentation, and online communication.

Why Accuracy Matters as Much as Speed

Typing quickly is helpful, but accuracy is equally important. Mistakes reduce real productivity because they interrupt your rhythm and require corrections later.

A person typing 50 WPM accurately may work more efficiently than someone typing 70 WPM with constant errors. That is why many typing tests measure both WPM and accuracy together.

How To Improve Typing Speed

  • Practice daily: short daily sessions help build consistency.
  • Focus on accuracy first: clean typing builds stronger long-term speed.
  • Use proper finger placement: efficient movement reduces wasted motion.
  • Take longer typing tests: longer practice helps build consistency and endurance.
  • Track your progress: compare WPM and accuracy over time.
  • Stay relaxed: tense hands and shoulders can slow typing down.

How Long Does It Take To Improve?

Typing improvement depends on practice habits, keyboard familiarity, and consistency. Many people notice improvement within a few weeks when they practice regularly.

Even practicing 5 to 15 minutes per day can make a noticeable difference over time. The key is consistency rather than extremely long practice sessions.
