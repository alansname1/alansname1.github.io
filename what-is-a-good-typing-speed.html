What Is a Good Typing Speed?

A good typing speed depends on your goals, experience, and how often you use a keyboard. For most people, typing around 40 words per minute (WPM) is considered average. With regular practice, many people improve to 60 WPM or higher, and advanced typists can reach 80 WPM or more.

Typing speed is not just about how fast you type. Accuracy is just as important. Typing quickly with many errors can slow you down overall because you spend more time correcting mistakes. A good typing speed combines both speed and accuracy.

Simple guideline: aim for steady improvement with strong accuracy instead of trying to reach a high WPM number too quickly.

Average Typing Speeds

Typing speed is often grouped into general ranges. These ranges can help you understand where you currently stand and what you might aim for next.

  • Beginner: 20–40 WPM
  • Average: 40–60 WPM
  • Advanced: 60–80 WPM
  • Expert: 80+ WPM

These numbers are not strict rules. Some people type slower but very accurately, while others type faster but make more mistakes. The best typing performance balances both.

What Typing Speed Do Jobs Require?

Many jobs require a minimum typing speed, especially roles that involve regular computer use. Common expectations include:

  • Basic office jobs: around 40 WPM
  • Customer support roles: 40–60 WPM
  • Data entry jobs: 50–70 WPM
  • Advanced typing roles: 70+ WPM

Employers also look at accuracy. A slightly lower WPM with strong accuracy is often better than high speed with frequent errors.

Why Typing Speed Matters

Faster typing can help you complete tasks more efficiently, reduce time spent on writing and editing, and improve overall productivity. This is useful for students, professionals, and anyone working on a computer regularly.

Typing speed also matters for communication. Whether you are writing emails, messages, or reports, being able to type comfortably makes the process smoother and less tiring.

How to Improve Your Typing Speed

Improving typing speed takes time, but the process is simple when you stay consistent.

  • Practice typing every day, even for short sessions
  • Focus on accuracy before trying to type faster
  • Use timed typing tests to measure progress
  • Avoid looking at the keyboard too often
  • Build comfortable and consistent typing habits

Regular practice helps your fingers learn the keyboard layout, which makes typing feel more natural over time.

How Long Does It Take to Improve Typing Speed?

Typing improvement depends on how often you practice and how focused your practice is. Many beginners notice small improvements within a few days, while larger improvements usually take a few weeks of consistent effort.

The most important factor is consistency. Practicing regularly, even for short periods, is more effective than practicing only once in a while.

How to Measure Your Typing Speed

The easiest way to measure typing speed is by taking a typing test. A typing test shows your words per minute and your accuracy, giving you a clear idea of your current skill level.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 40 WPM a good typing speed?

Yes, 40 WPM is considered average and is often enough for many jobs and everyday tasks.

What is a fast typing speed?

Typing above 60 WPM is generally considered fast, and speeds above 80 WPM are considered advanced.

Is accuracy more important than speed?

Yes. Accurate typing helps reduce errors and saves time, making it just as important as speed.

Can I improve typing speed quickly?

Yes, with daily practice and a focus on accuracy, many people see improvement within a few weeks.
