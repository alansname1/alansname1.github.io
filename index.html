What Is a Good Typing Speed?

A good typing speed for most people is around 40 words per minute (WPM). Many office jobs prefer 50 WPM or higher, while advanced typists can reach 70 to 100 WPM with consistent practice. The most important goal is not just speed, but typing cleanly and accurately.

How to Improve Typing Speed

The best way to improve typing speed is to practice regularly and focus on accuracy before trying to push for more speed. As your accuracy improves, your hands become more comfortable with common word patterns and your typing speed usually rises naturally.

  • Practice typing a little every day
  • Use timed typing tests to measure progress
  • Focus on fewer mistakes before chasing speed
  • Use longer typing tests to build stamina and rhythm

Frequently Asked Questions

What typing test should I start with?

A 1 minute typing test is a good starting point for quick checks and beginners. A 5 minute typing test is better if you want more realistic practice and a better measure of consistency.

Can typing practice help with jobs?

Yes. Typing practice can help with office work, administrative roles, customer support, data entry, and many work-from-home jobs that involve regular computer use.

How often should I practice?

Short daily practice is usually more effective than occasional long sessions. Even a few minutes a day can improve typing speed and accuracy over time.
