Measure More Than Speed

A useful typing test shows how quickly and accurately you can reproduce text. Your WPM score is important, but mistakes can reduce your effective performance.

  • WPM: your approximate words typed per minute.
  • Accuracy: how closely your typing matches the passage.
  • Errors: mistakes that interrupt rhythm and require corrections.
  • Consistency: your ability to maintain performance over time.

Typing Speed Benchmarks

Typing ability varies by experience, keyboard familiarity, and practice habits. These ranges are general guides rather than strict rules.

Typing SpeedGeneral LevelWhat It May Indicate
20–30 WPMBeginnerYou may still be learning key locations and basic rhythm.
35–45 WPMPractical averageYou can complete many common school, work, and computer tasks.
50–60 WPMGoodYou likely type comfortably for many everyday tasks.
70+ WPMStrongYou may handle typing-heavy tasks efficiently when accuracy is high.
90+ WPMAdvancedYou have developed strong speed, rhythm, and keyboard familiarity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a good typing speed?

Around 40 WPM is a practical average for many everyday tasks. A score of 50 to 60 WPM is often considered good, especially when accuracy is strong. Read the complete guide to good typing speed.

Should I practice speed or accuracy first?

Accuracy should usually come first. Fewer mistakes improve effective typing speed and make it easier to develop a smooth rhythm.

How often should I practice typing?

Ten to fifteen minutes per day is a realistic starting point. Consistency is generally more useful than an occasional long practice session.

Do I need to create an account?

No. The typing tests and educational resources are available without requiring an account.

Can typing practice help with jobs?

Yes. Many jobs involve email, chat support, forms, reports, data entry, or other keyboard-heavy tasks. See the typing jobs guide.
