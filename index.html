What Is a Typing Test?

A typing test measures how fast and accurately you can type. Your typing speed is calculated in words per minute (WPM), while accuracy shows how many words you typed correctly.

Typing tests are used by students, job seekers, office workers, and professionals to track progress and improve typing performance.

Typing tests are useful because they give you a measurable score instead of a guess. Rather than wondering whether your typing is improving, you can look at your WPM, accuracy, and consistency over time and see your progress clearly.

How to Improve Typing Speed

Improving typing speed takes practice, but small daily improvements can lead to big results. The best way to improve is to focus on accuracy first and speed second.

  • Focus on accuracy before speed
  • Practice 10 to 15 minutes daily
  • Use correct finger placement
  • Avoid looking at the keyboard

How Typing Tests Work

Typing tests measure both speed and accuracy. You are given text to type within a time limit, and your score is based on correct words per minute.

Most typing tests use a standard formula where one word equals five characters, including spaces.

Accuracy matters. Fewer mistakes lead to better overall typing performance.

Why Improving Typing Speed Matters

Typing faster helps with school, work, and daily computer use. It improves productivity and reduces frustration.

Strong typing skills are useful for writing, communication, and many online jobs.
