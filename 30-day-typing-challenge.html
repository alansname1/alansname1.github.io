How the 30-Day Challenge Works

This challenge gives you one focused typing activity for each day. Most sessions take about 10 to 20 minutes. The plan begins with accuracy and keyboard control, then gradually adds speed, endurance, and longer practice.

You can use the checkboxes to mark completed days. Your progress is saved automatically in your browser, so you can return later and continue where you stopped.

Main goal: build reliable typing habits first, then increase speed without losing accuracy.

Day 22 Type for 10 minutes with consistent accuracy and minimal pauses. Build endurance.

What To Do After Day 30

Finishing this challenge does not mean your typing practice has to stop. Use your results to choose the next goal.

  • If your accuracy is below your goal, continue slower accuracy practice.
  • If your accuracy is strong but your speed is low, add short timed tests.
  • If your speed drops during longer tests, continue endurance practice.
  • If certain letters or words cause mistakes, practice those patterns directly.
Recommended next step: repeat the challenge with a slightly higher WPM goal while keeping accuracy at 95% or better.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should I practice each day?

Most sessions in this challenge take about 10 to 20 minutes. You can shorten a session if your hands become tired or your concentration drops.

What accuracy should I aim for?

A useful goal is at least 95% accuracy. Beginners may need time to reach that level, so steady improvement matters more than perfection.

What happens if I miss a day?

Continue with the next unfinished day. You do not need to restart the entire challenge.

Should I repeat the same typing test?

Yes. Repeating the same test length makes it easier to compare results, but you should also practice varied text.

Will this challenge guarantee a certain WPM?

No. Improvement depends on your starting level, accuracy, consistency, technique, and practice habits. The challenge provides structure, not a guaranteed score.
