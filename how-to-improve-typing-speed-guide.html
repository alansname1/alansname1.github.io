What Does a Typing Test Measure?

WPM

Words per minute estimates how quickly you type during the test.

Accuracy

Accuracy shows how closely your typed text matches the sample passage.

Consistency

Longer tests show whether you can keep steady speed and accuracy.

Practice Progress

Repeated tests help you compare results and track improvement.

Why Typing Practice Matters

Typing is used in school, office work, remote jobs, customer service, data entry, writing, forms, email, and everyday computer tasks. Improving typing speed and accuracy can save time and reduce frustration.

A good typing test helps you measure your current ability. A good practice routine helps you improve. For many people, short daily typing practice is more effective than occasional long sessions.

Typing Speed Benchmarks

Typing Speed Level Meaning
20–30 WPM Beginner You are still building keyboard familiarity.
35–45 WPM Average Useful for many everyday computer tasks.
50–60 WPM Good Strong practical speed for school, work, and office tasks.
60–80 WPM Strong Efficient typing with good accuracy and rhythm.
80+ WPM Advanced Fast typing ability when accuracy is also high.

How To Improve Your Typing Test Score

  • Practice a few minutes every day.
  • Focus on accuracy before speed.
  • Use longer tests to build consistency.
  • Reduce how often you look at the keyboard.
  • Stay relaxed and avoid tense hands or shoulders.
  • Track your WPM and accuracy over time.
